How to Spot a Fake Alert
01
Check sender name. Real GTBank SMS comes from "GTBank", real Fidelity from "FidelitySMS" — never a phone number like 08012345678.
02
Look at account masking. Real alerts show **5117 or ****726 — never a full unmasked account number.
03
OPay/MoMo receipts are real if they show Transaction No. + Session ID. Fake generators usually skip these.
04
Spelling errors kill authenticity. "Creditted", "Recieved" — no Nigerian bank or fintech misspells official messages.
05
Always verify in your banking app. VerifyNG is a first filter — your bank app or USSD is the final word.
06
For amounts above ₦20,000 demand to see the live banking app on the sender's phone — not just a screenshot.
